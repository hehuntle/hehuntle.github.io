Microbial Enviorment Prediction

Analyzing time series of different microbial enviorments

Using data from a publication, the Bowman lab generated a neural net that would catergorize transitions in a microbial enviorment. The intial datalist used was from a scientist that took samples from his own saliva and fecal microbiome everyday over the course of a year. A self organizing map was trained to catorgorize the data into different ecosystems, otherwise known as modes. The practice of catorgizing data into modes is commonly used in industry. Throughout the time series the data transitions between the different modes. Through the normal net we are able to visualize the difference between the data collected during a steady mode, and the data collected during a ecosystem change.
